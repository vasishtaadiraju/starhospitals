Why Health Checks at Star Hospitals?

At Star Hospitals, our health checks go beyond basic evaluations. Employing state-of-the-art diagnostic equipment, our seasoned team of medical professionals provides you with an accurate, comprehensive health profile. Coupled with personalized consultations, we not only identify potential health risks but also guide you on lifestyle changes, making our health checks a genuinely holistic experience.

Early Detection of Health Issues
  • Health checks can detect potential health problems early, allowing for timely intervention and treatment
  • Early detection often leads to better outcomes and can prevent complications
Monitoring Health Progress
  • Regular health checks provide a baseline for monitoring your health over time and evaluating the effectiveness of lifestyle changes or treatments
  • They help track improvements or deteriorations in health and adjust management plans accordingly
Assessment of Vital Health Indicators
  • Health checks measure vital indicators such as cholesterol levels, blood sugar levels, Thyroid, Kidney and liver function to assess overall health and risk factors
  • These indicators are fundamental in understanding the body's functioning and potential health risks
Long-term Health Maintenance
  • Regular health checks contribute to a proactive approach to long-term health maintenance, aiming for a higher quality of life as individuals age
  • They support the management of chronic conditions and promote overall longevity and well-being

FAQ's

  • What types of health check-ups do you offer?

    We offer a wide range of health check-up packages including general health check-ups, specialized screenings for cardiac care, diabetes, and cancer, as well as age and gender-specific check-ups.

  • How often should one get a health check?

    A general health check-up should be performed at least once a year. For special cases, there will be a separate prescription from the doctor.

  • Who ought to consider undergoing health check-ups?

    Health check-ups are generally recommended for a wide range of individuals, although the type and frequency of screenings may vary based on age, sex, medical history, and risk factors.

  • What does a typical health check-up include?

    A general health check-up often includes a physical examination, blood tests, urine tests, x-rays, ultrasound and sometimes specialized screenings such as mammograms or colonoscopies depending on your age and risk factors.

  • At what age should routine health check-ups begin?

    Routine health check-ups should generally begin in early adulthood and continue regularly throughout life, with frequency and type of tests varying by age and risk factors.

  • Do women need special health check-ups, or are general ones sufficient?

    Women need both general health check-ups like blood pressure and cholesterol tests and gender-specific screenings like Pap smears and mammograms for conditions like cervical and breast cancer.
